# Customer Validation Guide

Use this guide to turn the investor MVP into validated product evidence. The goal is not to pitch the app first. The goal is to learn how people currently solve agistment and transport coordination, what hurts enough to pay for, and where PaddockME's workflow feels obviously better or obviously wrong.

## Interview Targets

Minimum before treating the personas as load-bearing:

- 5 livestock owners like Dale.
- 5 landowners like Brett or Lyn.
- 5 transport operators like Wayne or Sharon.
- 3 off-farm horse owners like Tash.

Prioritise people who have done agistment or stock transport in the last 24 months.

## Interview Rules

- Ask about the last real event, not opinions about a hypothetical product.
- Do not lead with "Would you use this?"
- Do not ask whether they like the design.
- Do not defend the product if they criticise it.
- Keep asking "what happened next?" until you have the real workflow.
- Capture exact words they use for paddocks, rates, transport, paperwork, and trust.
- End with the demo only after the current workflow is understood.

## Opening Script

"I'm working on PaddockME, a tool for coordinating agistment, land, and livestock transport. I do not want to sell you anything today. I want to understand the last time you had to find feed, offer paddocks, or move stock, and what made it painful."

## Core Questions For Everyone

1. Tell me about the last time you were involved in an agistment or stock movement.
2. Who started the process?
3. How did you find the other party?
4. What information did you need before saying yes?
5. What was written down, if anything?
6. What stayed in phone calls or text messages?
7. Where did the agreement nearly break down?
8. What paperwork mattered?
9. What did transport add to the problem?
10. What would have made the job less stressful?
11. What would you never trust software to decide for you?
12. Who paid whom, when, and how?
13. What would make you refuse to use a platform for this?
14. What would make you recommend one?

## Livestock Owner Questions

Use for Dale/Tash-style users.

1. What triggered the need for agistment?
2. How urgent was it?
3. How many calls/messages did it take to find somewhere suitable?
4. What made a paddock acceptable or unacceptable?
5. What did you need to know about feed, water, fencing, shade, yards, and access?
6. How did you judge whether the landowner was trustworthy?
7. What did you worry could go wrong after the stock arrived?
8. How did you arrange transport?
9. Did transport happen before or after agreement terms were settled?
10. What records did you need later?
11. What would you pay to avoid the same coordination work next time?

## Landowner Questions

Use for Brett/Lyn-style users.

1. When do you have spare paddock capacity?
2. How do people currently find out you have capacity?
3. What makes you cautious about taking outside stock?
4. What information do you require from the livestock owner?
5. What biosecurity checks matter?
6. How do you set or negotiate the rate?
7. How do you avoid tyre-kickers?
8. What would make incoming stock feel safe enough to accept?
9. What jobs do you not want to do once the stock arrive?
10. Would you prefer one longer agreement or multiple short ones?
11. What would payment need to look like before you trusted a platform?

## Transport Operator Questions

Use for Wayne/Sharon-style users.

1. Tell me about the last job where the return leg was empty.
2. How do you currently find backloads?
3. How much notice makes a job easier to plan?
4. What information do you need before quoting?
5. What paperwork gets duplicated?
6. Who usually causes delays: livestock owner, landowner, driver, yards, paperwork, timing?
7. What does a good transport request include?
8. What does a bad one leave out?
9. How do you price per head, per kilometre, per deck, or flat-rate jobs?
10. What payment terms are normal, and which ones hurt cash flow?
11. For fleet operators: what belongs in a profile once, and what belongs on each job?

## Demo Reactions To Watch

After the workflow interview, show the five-minute demo and watch for:

- Do they understand why agreement and transport are separate rooms?
- Do they trust the driver visibility boundary?
- Do they notice missing trust signals?
- Do they ask about payments before you mention them?
- Do they understand "Offer a paddock" without explanation?
- Do they see the transport backload value quickly?
- Do they ask whether it works on mobile?
- Do they compare it to Facebook, agents, saleyards, or spreadsheets?

## Strong Validation Signals

Look for:

- They describe a recent painful job without prompting.
- They name a specific person they would use this with.
- They ask to try it with a real upcoming job.
- They ask about price, payment, or onboarding.
- They volunteer missing fields that are operationally specific.
- They say the transport room/privacy split matches how they think.
- They can explain the product back to you in their own words.

## Weak Or Negative Signals

Watch for:

- "Looks nice" with no operational detail.
- They only want a listings board.
- They refuse written agreements.
- They do not see transport as connected to agistment.
- They do not trust unknown counterparties even with profiles.
- They say payments are the only thing that would matter.
- They would only use it if everyone else already used it.

## Evidence To Capture

For each interview, record:

- Persona type.
- Region.
- Livestock type.
- Last relevant event date.
- Current workflow steps.
- Number of calls/messages.
- Time from need to agreement.
- Whether transport was separate.
- Payment timing.
- Biggest trust blocker.
- Strongest quote.
- Product reaction.
- Follow-up permission.

## Scoring

Score each interview from 1 to 5:

- 1: No pain, no urgency, no fit.
- 2: Some pain, but not enough to change behaviour.
- 3: Clear pain, uncertain willingness to use/pay.
- 4: Strong pain and clear product pull.
- 5: Has an upcoming job and wants to trial the workflow.

Prioritise building around repeated 4s and 5s, not polite compliments.

## Post-Interview Synthesis

After every five interviews, update:

- the persona notes in `PADDOCKME_MASTER_SPEC.md` if a persona is wrong.
- `docs/reports/CURRENT_PRODUCT_AUDIT.md` if the built product no longer matches the real workflow.
- `docs/investor/INVESTOR_DILIGENCE_QA.md` if investor claims need sharpening.
- Product backlog only after patterns repeat.
