# Personas

Five named personas, ported from `WERKA 2026 / 03_Customer_Segments / 01_Customer_Persona_Profiles.docx` (3 May 2026).

## 1. Macca — the working tradesperson

- **Age:** mid-30s to mid-50s
- **Location:** suburban or peri-urban Australia, on the road across a metro region most days
- **Household:** sole trader or 1–2 employees; Ford Ranger or HiLux dual cab, trailer at home
- **Livestock context:** none directly — Macca is a tradesperson, not a farmer. Where he intersects PaddockME is when his trailer becomes useful for short stock moves, or when a mate asks him to drop a few head somewhere. Probably **not a primary persona for v1** but kept here for completeness.
- **What he needs (today's behaviour):** loads and unloads gear 4–8 times a day; treats logistics as friction to compress, not solve.
- **Friction he hits today:** ratchet straps fail or jam; webbing wears out invisibly; gear lives in a milk crate behind the seat; can't reliably teach an apprentice to tie a load.

## 2. Trev and Ros Robinson — the grey nomad couple

- **Age:** late 60s, retired
- **Location:** towing a 22-foot caravan around Australia at 90 km/h; 3+ months away from home each year
- **Household:** equity in a paid-off home + super of $700K–$1.5M; live frugally on the road, spend on quality where reliability matters
- **Livestock context:** none directly. The grey nomad pattern intersects PaddockME if the Robinsons own a small hobby property they leave for months at a time and need someone to take their few head while they're away — i.e. they could be **landowners with intermittent capacity**. Worth flagging for the landowner persona work in v1.
- **What they need:** repeatable, low-effort coordination they can both handle; reliability under remote conditions; clear written agreements they can settle from a caravan park.
- **Friction they hit today:** ratchet straps fail on corrugated roads; setup at each campsite is getting physically harder; the trucker's hitch is a skill Trev has but Ros doesn't; phone-tag from regional black spots is a daily reality.

## 3. Sam — the 4WD weekender

- **Age:** late 20s to mid 40s
- **Location:** metropolitan + Cape York / Vic High Country trips; modified Ranger or HiLux
- **Household:** salaried professional, junior tradie, or small-business owner earning $90k–$200k; spends seriously on vehicle setup as identity-as-much-as-utility
- **Livestock context:** unlikely as a livestock owner. Possible adjacency: **drover-curious** — a Sam-type with a stock crate and a long weekend could be a transport node in the network. To validate.
- **What he needs:** premium-tier gear that signals competence; gear that fits in a packed-out vehicle; brand cohort alignment with the rest of his kit.
- **Friction he hits today:** existing tie-downs feel cheap next to his $5,000 of recovery gear; vehicle damage from over-tensioning; storage real estate is constant.

## 4. Jen — the home-handy DIY-er

- **Age:** mid-30s to early 50s
- **Location:** suburban or peri-urban Australia
- **Household:** salaried professional / public servant / healthcare / teacher earning $65k–$130k; partner + 1–2 kids; hatchback or compact SUV with roof rails
- **Livestock context:** none directly. Could be a **property-owner-with-paddocks** persona if the household has acreage and 1–2 horses, alpacas, or sheep — i.e. small-scale lifestyle farmers. Worth a separate persona pass.
- **What she needs:** confidence; tools that make her competent the first time without YouTube tutorials.
- **Friction she hits today:** has fought with the trucker's hitch four times from a YouTube tutorial; tangled gear in milk crates; genuinely worried about things coming loose; doesn't feel competent with rope.

## 5. Dale — the regional / farming customer

- **Age:** 40s to 70s
- **Location:** on or near a rural property
- **Household:** runs a small-to-mid family farm, a contracting business serving farms, or works as a regional government / utility employee; income $70k–$200k; fleet of 2 utes + 1–2 trailers + quad bike + older Land Cruiser; shops at Mitre 10, Elders, Nutrien Ag Solutions, CRT, ProAg
- **Livestock context (the core PaddockME persona):** Dale is the **farmer-with-livestock** archetype. Owns or manages cattle, sheep, horses, or goats. Has paddocks. Knows what good pasture looks like. Treats every storm warning as a personal logistics event. Has done agistment by phone and saleyard-handshake for 30 years.
- **What he needs:** a way to find spare paddock space outside his own district when he's dry, without 14 phone calls; written agreement with rates, duration, feed, water, and fencing in one place; a reliable way to find a stock-crate driver going his way on the right day; a record of where his livestock are and how they're going.
- **Friction he hits today:** verbal agreements that lead to disputes about rates, feed, water, and how long the stock will be there; transport is a separate ring-around; no central record of where the livestock are or how they're going; storms and weather windows are unforgiving.

## How to use this doc

When considering a feature or screen, ask: **Would Dale's life get easier?** If the answer is yes, build it. If the answer is no, ask whether it serves a livestock-owner / landowner / drover persona we haven't formalised yet — and if not, defer.
